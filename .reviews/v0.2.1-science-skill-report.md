Note: fitted EC50=1.106 mg/L (line 111) differs materially from Hamberg's native ~4.6 mg/L, but this is disclosed in-line at lines 102-109 as a fitted-to-outputs value that produces INR=2.5 at 5 mg/day steady-state; this is defensible as long as the paper's §8 framing is honored (it is).

T1.2 IWPC 2009 — PASS. Paper §11 does NOT claim IWPC pharmacogenetic implementation (correction applied in v0.2.1). IWPC values are used only as calibration TARGETS: `published_calibration.json` benchmark `iwpc_mean_maintenance_dose=5.2 mg/day` and `iwpc_days_to_therapeutic=7.0 days` match IWPC 2009 cohort summaries.

T1.3 Raschke 1993 nomogram — PASS. `src/hemosim/agents/baselines.py:62-91` (HeparinRaschkeBaseline) docstring explicitly frames the modern ACCP absolute-seconds formulation with 1.5-2.5× ratio equivalence and cites Raschke 1993 + Hirsh 2001 + Holbrook 2012. T1.8 ISTH Schulman 2005 major bleeding — PASS. `clinical.py:184-245` implements the four qualifying criteria (fatal, critical_site, Hb drop ≥ 2.0 g/dL, transfusion ≥ 2 units) per Schulman/Kearon 2005 J Thromb Haemost definition. Thresholds hard-coded at lines 180-181.

T1.9 Warkentin 4T HIT score — PASS. `clinical.py:316-420` implements four categorical components: thrombocytopenia (>50% fall + nadir≥20 → 2 pts), timing (5-10 days → 2 pts, 10-14 → 1 pt), thrombosis (binary 0/2 pts), other causes (none=2/possible=1/definite=0). T1.10 Hockin-Mann 2002 reduced cascade — **FAIL-with-caveat**. `src/hemosim/models/coagulation.py` species list (TF:VIIa, Xa, Va, IIa/thrombin, fibrinogen, fibrin, AT-III-bound, platelet-act) is the standard Hockin-Mann reduction. Rate constants at lines 28-62 are physiologically plausible order-of-magnitude (k_thrombin_gen=1.0 /(nM·min), prothrombin=1400 nM). **However: live test with TF:VIIa=1.0 + Xa=0.5 + Va=0.5 + fibrinogen=300 stimulus for 120 minutes produced thrombin=0.00, fibrin=0.002** — the cascade does not actually generate thrombin on any tested trigger. Mass conservation (fibrinogen + fibrin) holds perfectly at 300 mg/dL because the formation rate is ~0. Paper §15 has a general "intermediate species dynamics may matter" caveat but does not disclose the specific "thrombin never exceeds 0 nM in the reduced model even with full trigger" failure mode. A biology-literate reviewer who runs a standalone cascade simulation will notice inside 15 minutes.

T1.11 Holbrook 2012 dose caps — PASS. `src/hemosim/clinical/safety.py:158-168` HEPARIN_SAFETY_BOUNDS: max 25 U/kg/hr (conservative margin above Raschke 22 U/kg/hr per docstring line 118-120), 80 U/kg bolus, 1 h refractory (cited to Hirsh 2001 distribution t½). WARFARIN_SAFETY_BOUNDS elsewhere in same file tie to Holbrook 2012 INR management. No fabricated institutional caps.

T1.12 Nemati 2016 DQN reimplementation — PASS-with-note. `src/hemosim/agents/baselines_extended.py:90-120` uses 2-layer MLP (64 hidden units, ReLU) with 5 discrete actions {-4, -2, 0, +2, +4} U/kg/hr adjustments matching Nemati 2016 §II-C action grid description. The 2016 IEEE EMBC paper does not specify hidden-layer width, so 64 is an inference and properly caveated.

T1.13 Bowen / Lippi / Whitehead lab CVs — PASS. `src/hemosim/envs/pomdp.py:185-187` aPTT CV=0.08 (Lippi 2012 reports aPTT CV 5-10%), anti-Xa CV=0.12 (Lippi 10-15%), platelets CV=0.05 (Lippi 3-6%).
