# hemosim Translation Pathway

**Phase 1 (now — v0.2.2 released):** Open-source simulation infrastructure + published-data calibration paper. Four Gymnasium-compatible environments grounded in published PK/PD models; POMDP reformulation with explicit lab ordering and analytical variability; reduced 8-state Hockin-Mann cascade ODE integrated into the DIC environment as a physiological scaffold (scoped to DIC pathophysiology — AT-III-depleted, intense upstream triggering — per §6 and `.reviews/mechanistic-claims.md::dic_hockin_mann_cascade`); clinical-outcome metrics (Rosendaal TTR, ISTH major bleeding, Warkentin 4T HIT, mortality proxy); published-data calibration with normalized RMSE 0.0013 on warfarin against Hamberg/IWPC and documented residuals on heparin against Raschke/Hirsh/Wan; CDS harness with rule-based safety layer; SPIRIT-2013-compliant silent-deployment observational trial protocol; faithful reimplementation of the Nemati 2016 DQN as a shared baseline. Released under MIT license as `pip install hemosim`, ships 383 unit + integration + integrity-audit + SR-M falsification tests. Every mechanistic claim in the paper is registered in `.reviews/mechanistic-claims.md` and has a falsification test that runs under the artifact's default initialization and asserts against a primary-source numeric range.

**Phase 2 (0–12 months):** Individual-patient MIMIC-IV calibration + RL policy training. Per-patient PK/PD parameter fitting against MIMIC-IV aPTT and INR trajectories; re-run of the calibration harness at the individual-patient level; PPO and adjacent RL algorithms trained on the patient-calibrated environment; head-to-head comparison against the Nemati 2016 DQN reimplementation. Deliverable: a companion paper reporting policy reward curves, TTR on held-out patient cohorts, and hyperparameters + results emitted to `results/hyperparameters.json` + `results/training_results.json` (currently absent — scope-excluded from Phase 1). PhysioNet credentialed access to MIMIC-IV is a precondition. Primary collaborator target: an academic Predictive Health Analytics group with existing MIMIC-IV credentialed access whose prior published work includes deep-RL anticoagulation dosing (e.g., the group behind the Nemati 2016 IEEE EMBC paper) or a peer group with equivalent infrastructure.

**Phase 3 (12–24 months):** Silent deployment under the SHADOW-HEP protocol at a partner ICU. Locked policy surfaces recommendations to a research database without influencing patient management; primary endpoint Rosendaal TTR on aPTT with a 10-percentage-point non-inferiority margin; DSMB composition specified in `paper/silent_deployment_protocol.tex`; stopping trigger is a counterfactual bleed-proximity rule; enrollment 350 patients (up to 500) over 6–12 months. Anticipated manuscript target: *The Lancet Digital Health* or *NEJM AI*. Partner institution selection is a Phase 2 ask to domain-expert collaborators.

**Phase 4 (24–36 months):** Prospective multi-site randomized controlled trial. Clinician-directed care vs. RL-augmented care with recommendations surfaced to providers. ClinicalTrials.gov registration mandatory; IRB requires full informed consent. Funding target: an NIH / AHRQ / ARPA-H mechanism that supports multi-site prospective AI-in-healthcare trials.

**Phase 5 (36–48 months):** Regulatory pathway toward Software-as-a-Medical-Device status under the FDA AI/ML SaMD Action Plan. Specific pathway (510(k), De Novo, or PMA) depends on IMDRF risk classification, which is a function of the declared intended use and the state of the healthcare situation; not pre-committed at Phase 1. The `SafeDSS` safety layer and uncertainty-aware deferral in hemosim (§13) are designed to make the device's output conservative and auditable, which should be favorable under most reasonable risk-classification analyses. Regulatory counsel is engaged at the intended-use-statement stage.

---

**Capital strategy:** Non-dilutive grants carry Phases 2 and 3; equity-funded spinout or licensing deal carries Phases 4 and 5. Phase 1 was self-funded under the STI Research Institute brand.

**Non-dilutive targets (Phase 2):** NIH R01 (NHLBI hematologic conditions; NIGMS pharmacokinetics), AHRQ R18 (decision-support implementation), ARPA-H (AI sim-to-real in healthcare), VA HSR&D (anticoagulation is a major VA operational burden).

**Defensibility:** The clinical validation + regulatory clearance layers are hard for open-source replication. The open-source Phase-1 artifact is the "free" layer that establishes credibility; validated per-patient calibrated policies, the silent-deployment dataset, and eventual FDA clearance are the paid / proprietary layers.

**Current phase status:** Phase 1 (v0.2.2) is published and pre-outreach. SR-M gate family is green; review certificate status is APPROVED FOR PUBLICATION.

**Next collaborator ask:** A domain-expert co-investigator with MIMIC-IV credentialed access to serve as clinical PI (or co-PI) on a Phase-2 R01 submission, with hemosim as the simulation substrate and the Phase-2 paper as the joint deliverable. The existing Nemati 2016 citation chain and the Wan-2008 aPTT-TTR target framing make this a natural extension of published predictive-health-analytics work.
